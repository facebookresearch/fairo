Tell Us What Is In The Scene

Navigating the Scene:

Click once within the window to get everything inside to fully render. You can use the arrow keys to rotate the view, or click and drag. You can also zoom with your scroll wheel.

Block Colors:

The "ground" in the scene is made up of white blocks. The objects in the scene can be made up of any other color blocks and have darker edges. The "air" in the scene is transparent.

The Task:

Your task is to tell us one (just one!) "thing" from scene in the text box below it. Some examples of things you could name are:
  • An object, like "cube" or "house"
  • Part of an object, like "corner", "edge", "side", etc.
  • A combination of objects, like "the two holes" or
  • An object with a reference to another object, like "the space between the cube and the wall"

Separate Object and Location:

Please submit your answer in two parts, first just the object and then where it is in the scene. Here are some examples:
  • "A blue cube above the ground" -> Object: "blue cube", Location: "above the ground"
  • "A small step near a big blue ball" -> Object: "small step", Location: "near a big blue ball"
  • "A green ball and a purple walkway next to each other" -> Object: "green ball", Location: "next to a purple walkway" or vice versa

Things Not To Do:

In order to pass the HIT successfully, please keep the following in mind:
  • Please only submit one object
  • Don't respond with any profanity or violent words
  • The object must refer to an object made of non-white blocks, so not "the air", "the ground", "space", "a scene", etc.
  • Please submit a new answer each time if you do the HIT multiple times.
  • Try to be creative. Think of interesting or non-obvious things to say.
When you are finished with the HIT, please answer the questions at the bottom and click 'Submit'.
